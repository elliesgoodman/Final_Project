The Thirteenth East Wing Biennial

SURGE represents the vibrancy and talent of emerging artists studying in London Today


In 1991 Joshua Compston began The East Wing Collection to restore art to "it's rightful position" in the seminar rooms of The Courtauld Institute of Art. The biennial exhibition, now in it's thirteenth edition, continues to do just that. This year's exhibition has been a fantastic opportunity to build something out of tradition, aware of its history and ambitious for its future. The works on display grapple with what the traditions of art mean in the present and what role gender plays in students' futures. Two major themes have emerged in the selections, as discussed by Claire and Aileen in their insightful essays in our catalogue. Tradition and gender preoccupy many of the artists whose mediums and concepts explore and challenge past definitions to discuss what is worth holding onto for the future.
